The hands they’re in

Hi, I’m Rosie

I’ve been grooming dogs for over twelve years, and I opened Barker & Bloom because I wanted a calmer kind of salon: one dog at a time, no rush, no chaos.

I’m calm-handling trained, which really just means I read what your dog is telling me and go at their pace. Nervous dogs, older dogs, wriggly puppies. They’re all welcome, and they all leave a little happier than they came in.

“I treat every dog like it’s my own. That’s the whole job, really.”

A word with Rosie

Ask away

The questions we hear most. Tap one to see Rosie’s answer.

How often should my dog be groomed?
RFor most dogs it’s every 6–8 weeks, though it depends on the coat: curly and long coats need a little more, short coats a little less. Book once and I’ll remind you when your dog’s due, so you never have to keep a mental note.
My dog is nervous, or it’s their first groom
RThen you’ve come to the right place. I keep the salon quiet, take everything at your dog’s pace, and never force anything. For puppies and first-timers there’s a short, gentle intro groom so it’s a good first experience. Just tell me on the booking form what they’re like and I’ll plan around it.
Do you groom cats?
RYes, by request, with a few calm slots each week. Cats need a different, careful approach, so I arrange these over the phone first for a proper chat. Cat grooming starts from £35. Give me a ring on 07700 900123.
What’s included in a full groom?
RA warm bath with the right shampoo for their coat, a blow-dry and full brush-out, a clip or scissor-style to breed or your preference, nails trimmed, ears cleaned and checked, and a light finishing spritz. The works, basically.
How long does it take?
RUsually between 1½ and 3 hours depending on size, coat and how relaxed your dog is. I don’t double-book or rush, so I’d always rather take a little longer and keep your dog comfortable.
What if my dog’s coat is matted?
RI’ll never hurt a dog to save a coat. If matting is tight, the kindest thing is often a shorter clip to start fresh, and I’ll always show you and talk it through first. From there, regular grooms keep it easy and comfortable.
Is there a first-visit offer?
RThere is! Book your dog’s first groom online and you’ll get £5 off. Just let me know it’s their first visit when you book.
